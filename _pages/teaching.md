## Teaching Philosophy

My teaching practice is student-centred and project-based, designed to 
cultivate both technical proficiency and creative autonomy. I prioritise 
the introduction of diverse paradigms and perspectives, encouraging students 
to engage critically with a wide range of musical and technological 
traditions. Rather than prescribing a single approach, I tailor assignments 
and projects to each student's strengths and developmental stage, 
challenging them to expand their creative and intellectual horizons.

## Graduate Supervision

As of June 2026, I have supervised **11 master's theses** 
to completion. Topics include:

- An Analysis and Interpretation of "Im Treibhaus" and "Träume" from Wagner's Wesendonck Lieder, and the Relation to Tristan und Isolde.
- An Analysis and Collaborative Piano Interpretation of Yu-Ling Lin’s Six Selected Choral Works
- Re-creation of Cantonese *Xianshui Ge* (鹹水歌)
- Modelling of Hakka *Huazhao Xi* (花朝戲)
- Accessibility research on electronic wind instruments 
  for elderly users
- Visualisation programming of *Erhu* works in TouchDesigner
- Application of *Erhu* timbral granular synthesis in composition
